Frequently Asked Questions

If a unit has hand sanitizer, do I still need a separate handwashing station?

While sanitizer is great, handwashing with soap and water is more effective at removing certain types of germs and physical grime. For events with food or children, we highly recommend adding handwashing stations for complete hygiene.

What do you do to control odors, especially in the summer heat?

We use a powerful, non-formaldehyde deodorizing solution in the tank that neutralizes odors at the source. We also include an odor-absorbing disc in each unit, which works to keep the interior fresh.

Are your cleaning chemicals safe for the environment and for people with sensitivities?

Yes. We have specifically chosen eco-friendly, biodegradable cleaning agents and tank solutions that are powerful on germs but safe for the environment and gentle enough to not cause irritation.

Do I need a permit from the city to place a porta potty on my property?

For private property, a permit is typically not required. If the unit needs to be placed on public property, like a street or sidewalk, a permit may be necessary. We can advise you on local regulations.

Can you place a unit on a surface that isn't perfectly level, like my driveway?

Our drivers are trained to find the most level and stable position possible. We use shims and blocks to ensure the unit is secure and safe to use, even on slight inclines.

Gloucester City, NJ Insights: Population,
Zip Codes, Influence, and Service Areas

Gloucester City is a city in Camden County, in the U.S. state of New Jersey. As of the 2020 United States census, the city's population was 11,484, an increase of 28 (+0.2%) from the 2010 census count of 11,456, which in turn reflected a decline of 28 (−0.2%) from the 11,484 counted in the 2000 census. It is located directly across the Delaware River from Philadelphia and the Port of Philadelphia.
